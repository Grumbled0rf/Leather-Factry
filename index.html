Our Story

A house built from desert and patience.

EBL began in a single workshop on the edge of the Liwa desert — one master, three apprentices, and a conviction that the camel hide, long overlooked, deserved to stand among the world's great leathers. Today our artisans honour that first stitch in every piece they finish.

We tan slowly with vegetable matter. We cut by hand. We refuse the shortcut. The result is not fashion. It is inheritance — leather that softens with each decade, carrying the hand of its maker and the warmth of the country that raised it.

The Craft

The Art of Making

Every EBL piece passes through six pairs of hands across as many weeks. A slow grammar of choices — each one quiet, each one consequential.

Hide Selection
Only the finest by-product hides from local Emirati farms — graded for grain, weight and integrity by our master tanner.
Vegetable Tanning
Twelve weeks in pits of bark, sumac and date — a heritage process that yields a leather that warms and deepens with age.
Hand Cutting
No die-press, no compromise. Each panel is read for its grain and cut by an artisan who has trained for over a decade.
Master Finishing
Edges painted in seven coats. Saddle-stitched at eight per inch. Burnished by hand until the leather sings.
Inspection
Three independent reviews before a piece leaves the atelier. If a single stitch is uncertain, the piece returns to the bench.
Signature
Each finished piece is hand-numbered and signed by its maker — a covenant between artisan and owner across the years.
